# 9. Between Glitch Aesthetics and Post-Perception: Technical Error or Sensory Failure?

## Comparative Study of Two Approaches to Error

**Ibrahim Shalabi**

---

## Abstract

This paper compares Glitch Aesthetics and Post-Perception—two contemporary movements engaging with error and failure. Glitch Aesthetics locates error in the **technical medium** (malfunction of hardware, software, data transmission). Post-Perception locates error in **human perception** (fragility of the senses). Through comparative analysis, the paper demonstrates these movements address different levels: Glitch Aesthetics reveals machine vulnerability; Post-Perception reveals human vulnerability.

---

## 1. Glitch Aesthetics

### Definition
An artistic movement using digital error (malfunction, corruption, distortion) as an aesthetic and critical resource.

### Source of Error
Technical medium: hardware failure, software crash, data corruption.

### Aesthetic Value
The beauty of breakdown; poetics of failure.

### Goal
Reveal the hidden materiality and fragility of digital systems.

---

## 2. Post-Perception

### Definition
An artistic movement testing the fragility of human perception.

### Source of Error
Human perception: sensory contradiction, expectation violation, processing overload.

### Aesthetic Value
Productive discomfort; perceptual disruption.

### Goal
Train the perceiver to resist deception.

---

## 3. Key Differences

### What Fails
- Glitch Aesthetics: **The machine**
- Post-Perception: **The perceiver**

### Type of Error
- Glitch Aesthetics: Event (temporary malfunction)
- Post-Perception: Condition (ongoing vulnerability)

### Technical State
- Glitch Aesthetics: Broken system
- Post-Perception: Perfectly functioning system

---

## 4. Complementarity

Both movements address errors at different levels:
- Glitch Aesthetics: System-level failure
- Post-Perception: Perceiver-level failure

Together, they reveal the full picture of digital culture: machines are fragile AND perceivers are vulnerable.

---

## 5. Conclusion

Glitch Aesthetics and Post-Perception are complementary responses to digital culture's crises. One reveals the machine's fragility; the other reveals the perceiver's fragility. Both are necessary.
