# 10. Post-Perception and the Avant-Garde Movements: From Dada to Conceptual Art

## Genealogy, Continuities, and Ruptures

**Ibrahim Shalabi**

---

## Abstract

This paper situates Post-Perception within the genealogy of twentieth-century avant-garde movements, from Dada to Conceptual Art. Post-Perception inherits from Dada the critique of meaning and shock; from Surrealism the exploration of the irrational; from Conceptual Art the primacy of the idea. However, Post-Perception ruptures with these movements by shifting the target of critique from the artwork to **perception itself**, transforming the viewer from spectator to **laboratory subject**, and introducing **perceptual immunity** as the purpose of art.

---

## 1. Dada: Critique of Meaning

### Inheritance
- Shock as a tool
- Language deconstruction
- Anti-institutional stance

### Rupture
- Dada destroyed meaning; Post-Perception reconstructs perception
- Dada was nihilistic; Post-Perception is constructive

---

## 2. Surrealism: Exploration of the Irrational

### Inheritance
- Symbolic and mythical dimensions
- Irrational juxtaposition
- Exploration of what transcends reason

### Rupture
- Surrealism liberated the unconscious; Post-Perception trains conscious perception
- Surrealism looked inward; Post-Perception looks outward to the senses

---

## 3. Conceptual Art: Primacy of the Idea

### Inheritance
- Idea more important than object
- Dematerialization
- Language as medium

### Rupture
- Conceptual Art asked "What is art?"; Post-Perception asks "How do we perceive?"
- Conceptual Art was about the artwork; Post-Perception is about the viewer

---

## 4. Postmodernism: Deconstruction

### Inheritance
- Deconstruction of fixed meanings
- Reflexivity and self-critique
- Skepticism toward master narratives

### Rupture
- Postmodernism was ironic; Post-Perception is committed
- Postmodernism deconstructed without reconstructing; Post-Perception offers reconstruction (perceptual immunity)

---

## 5. The Radical Break

### What Post-Perception Adds
No earlier avant-garde proposed **perceptual immunity**—the capacity to resist perceptual deception through training.

### Historical Justification
Each avant-garde responded to its historical moment:
- Dada: WWI trauma
- Surrealism: Bourgeois repression
- Conceptual Art: Commodification of art
- Postmodernism: Collapse of master narratives
- Post-Perception: **Crisis of perception in the age of simulation**

---

## 6. Conclusion

Post-Perception is both heir to the avant-garde tradition and a radical break from it. It inherits the avant-garde's commitment to challenging conventions but directs this challenge at a new target: perception itself.
