# 12. Criteria for Evaluating a Post-Perception Artwork: Toward Experimental Criticism

## Nine Criteria with Measurable Indicators

**Ibrahim Shalabi**

---

## Abstract

This paper proposes nine evaluation criteria for assessing Post-Perception artworks. Unlike traditional art criticism (focused on formal qualities, aesthetic pleasure, originality), these criteria measure **perceptual efficacy**: Does the work disrupt perception? Does it generate perceptual immunity? Drawing on experimental psychology, behavioral observation, and phenomenological analysis, the paper provides both qualitative frameworks and quantitative metrics for evaluation.

---

## Nine Evaluation Criteria

### Criterion 1: Effectiveness of Perceptual Disruption (15% weight)
**Question:** Does the work successfully disrupt automatic perceptual processing?

**Indicators:**
- Momentary surprise (facial expression analysis)
- Expectation violation (self-report)
- Sensory contradiction (observable)
- Depth of disruption (temporal persistence)

### Criterion 2: Cognitive Engagement (15% weight)
**Question:** Does the work engage the viewer intellectually?

**Indicators:**
- Question generation
- Multiple interpretations
- Connection to prior experience
- Analytical reflection

### Criterion 3: Viewer Participation (10% weight)
**Question:** Does the viewer have an active role?

**Levels:**
- 0: Passive viewing
- 1: Simple physical interaction
- 2: Intellectual engagement
- 3: Existential decision
- 4: Active contribution
- 5: Co-creation

### Criterion 4: Temporal Persistence (15% weight)
**Question:** Does the effect linger after the experience?

**Indicators:**
- Spontaneous recall (days later)
- Behavioral change
- Talking about the work
- Re-evaluation over time

### Criterion 5: Transferability (10% weight)
**Question:** Does the work produce similar effects across contexts?

**Indicators:**
- Cultural independence
- Translatability
- Multiple readings
- Comparative testing

### Criterion 6: Philosophical Depth (15% weight)
**Question:** Does the work raise profound questions?

**Levels:**
- 0: Superficial
- 1: Technical
- 2: Social
- 3: Psychological
- 4: Perceptual
- 5: Existential

### Criterion 7: Meta-Critical Awareness (10% weight)
**Question:** Does the work acknowledge its own limits?

**Indicators:**
- Awareness of complicity
- Tool critique
- Openness to critique
- Avoidance of dogma

### Criterion 8: Perceptual Immunity Generated (10% weight)
**Question:** Does the work contribute to immunity against deception?

**Indicators:**
- Sensory vigilance
- Critical deconstruction
- Constructive skepticism
- Perceptual memory

### Criterion 9: Openness to Experimentation (5% weight)
**Question:** Does the work remain open to new interpretations?

**Indicators:**
- Unfinishedness
- Generative potential
- Resistance to closure
- Invitation to future artists
